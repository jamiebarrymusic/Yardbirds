Community & policy work

Building the movement, not just the coops

Khrysti has been an active part of the urban agriculture policy conversation in Massachusetts since the early days of YBC — helping to shape the rules that make backyard chickens possible for thousands of people.

Somerville urban agriculture ordinance

Khrysti was an integral part of the creation of Somerville's urban agriculture-focused zoning ordinance — the first of its kind in the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

City of Boston — Article 89

Following Somerville's lead, Khrysti worked with the City of Boston to help develop Article 89, Boston's urban agriculture rezoning — opening the door for backyard chickens and urban farming across the city.

Teaching & public appearances

Khrysti has presented at the Boston Flower & Garden Show, Northeastern University, Tufts Vet School, Mass Audubon, the Urban Farming Institute, countless farmers' markets, garden clubs, libraries, and community events across the region.

The Chickeness

Khrysti Smyth Barry

Keeping chickens since May 2009
Teaching classes & seminars since 2012
Insured & bonded pet sitter

Khrysti started thinking about keeping her own backyard birds in the fall of 2008 and quickly soaked up everything she could find — hours of research on coop design, breeds, and every aspect of their care. She got her first batch of chicks in May 2009 and never looked back.

Over the next few years she became deeply involved in the broader regional community of backyard chicken hobbyists — entering poultry shows with a few of her birds, getting to know breeders and keepers across New England, and beginning to teach classes and seminars in the Boston area. By late spring of 2012 it was clear that her knowledge and her position within the community were in high demand, and Yardbirds Backyard Chickens was... ahem... hatched.

Since then, Khrysti has become a go-to resource for urban chicken keepers across New England and beyond. She has taught hundreds of classes, consulted on countless coop builds, and helped new flock owners navigate the whole journey from "is this even legal?" to "why won't she go into the coop at night?" (It's always that one hen.)

In late 2018, YBC's home base migrated from the Boston area out to western Massachusetts — to the delightful, farm-y town of Greenfield, MA in the Pioneer Valley, where Khrysti lives with her flock and menagerie.
